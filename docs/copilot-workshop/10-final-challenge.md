# 10 — Final Challenge: Copilot Showdown

> ⏱️ 15 minutes | 🏆 Team challenge — most impressive thing wins

---

## Setup (2 min)

> "Final challenge. Form teams of 2-3. You have 10 minutes. The rules are simple:"

**The Rules:**
1. Build the most impressive thing you can using Copilot
2. It has to actually work (no fake demos)
3. You'll have 60 seconds to demo it to the room
4. The room votes on the winner

**That's it. No other constraints.** Language, framework, topic — completely open.

> "This is the final exam, and the only thing being graded is 'did you make the room go wow?'"

---

## Inspiration (For Teams That Need a Nudge)

If a team looks stuck after 1 minute, offer these prompts:

- "Build a terminal-based game — Wordle clone, hangman, 2048, tic-tac-toe with an AI opponent"
- "Build a CLI tool that generates random excuses for being late to standup"
- "Build an ASCII art generator that converts text to big letters"
- "Build a code review bot that roasts your code (humorously)"
- "Build a regex explainer that takes a regex and outputs what it matches in plain English"
- "Build a commit message generator that reads your git diff and suggests a commit message"
- "Build a mock interview question generator for a specific tech stack"

---

## Timer

Put a big visible countdown on screen. Play some background music if you've got speakers — keep the energy up.

Call out time remaining at 5 minutes, 2 minutes, and 30 seconds.

---

## Demos (5 min)

> "Time! Let's see what you've got."

Each team gets **60 seconds** max. Keep it tight.

**How to run it:**
1. Team name / members (5 sec)
2. What they built (10 sec)
3. Live demo (45 sec)

After each demo, ask the room for applause level (informal voting).

---

## Award the Winner (1 min)

> "And the winner of the first-ever [Your Org Name] Copilot Showdown is..."

Prize ideas (low-cost, high-fun):
- GitHub stickers / swag (if available)
- "First pick of lunch spot" for the team
- A ridiculous trophy (print a GitHub Copilot logo on a paper plate)
- Bragging rights and a photo for Slack/Teams

**Celebrate everyone:**
> "Seriously though — look what everyone in this room built today. Four hours ago, some of you had never used Copilot. Now you're building games and APIs in 10 minutes. That's the real win."
